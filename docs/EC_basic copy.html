10/22 Morning Opening Talks

Tropical peatlands cover ~21% of global peatland area

Oil Palm Plantations & Peatlands

- Oil Palm trees require consistently moist, deep, and well-drained soil rich in humus. If planted oil palm trees on peatlands, which could 1) Disturb peatlands by drying lands before plantations and 2) Cause water flooding

From Kristell Hergoualc’h: A conversion of forest peatlands to oil palm trees could release ~296 Mg CO2-C /ha/5yr

14:40 - 14:50 Bayas Room

Talk 4 - 14:40 Jing Huang (10 min): “Drought-driven changes in microbial carbon use efficiency are regulated by POC and microbial life history strategy in a subtropical forest”

Hypotheses: 1) Drought decrease soil MCUE, magnitude of the effect varying by soil depths -> drought-induced changes in MCUE were depth-dependent, with opposite responses in surface (0-15 cm) and subsoil (0-30cm)…proteobacteria behaved differently, distinct pattern on soil -depths MCUE

2) Substrate availability is the dominant driver controlling MCUE under drought stress-> POC and microbial lefe history strategy regulate drought effects on microbial CUE

3) Shifts in microbial community structure, particularly the ratio of Y- to A- strategies, govern the CUE responses by fundamentally altering resource allocation pattern under water limitation -> Among microbial taxa, ptotebacteria is most crucial for MCUE
